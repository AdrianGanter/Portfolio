# SOC Role in Blue Team – Key Concepts

## Security Operations Center (SOC)
A SOC is a centralised team responsible for monitoring, detecting, investigating, and responding to cyber security incidents within an organisation.

It acts as the first line of defence, handling security alerts and coordinating responses to threats.

### SOC Structure
A typical SOC includes:
- **L1 Analysts** – First responders who triage alerts and escalate complex cases
- **L2 Analysts** – Perform deeper investigations and incident analysis
- **SOC Engineers** – Build and maintain detection tools, SIEM rules, and security infrastructure
- **SOC Manager** – Oversees operations, reporting, and team coordination

---

## Blue Team
Blue Team refers to all defensive cyber security functions within an organisation.

Core responsibilities:
- Monitoring systems for malicious activity
- Incident detection and response
- Threat mitigation and recovery
- Improving security posture over time

---

## Cyber Security Organisational Structure

### Key Leadership Role
- **CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)**  
  Responsible for overall cyber security strategy and decision-making at the executive level

---

### Security Departments

#### Red Team
Offensive security specialists who simulate attacks to identify vulnerabilities.

#### Blue Team
Defensive security teams responsible for detecting and responding to threats.

Includes:
- SOC teams
- Incident response teams
- Security engineers

#### GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance)
Responsible for:
- Security policies
- Regulatory compliance
- Risk management frameworks (e.g. ISO standards)

---

## Cyber Incident Response Team (CIRT)
Specialist team that handles high-severity or complex incidents.

Includes:
- Digital forensics analysts
- Threat hunters
- Malware analysts
- Incident responders

Used when incidents exceed SOC capability or require deep investigation.

---

## MSSP vs Internal SOC

### Internal SOC
- Dedicated to one organisation
- Fewer tools and systems
- More stable workload
- Deep familiarity with environment

### MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider)
- Provides SOC services to multiple clients
- High alert volume and fast-paced environment
- Broad tool exposure
- More varied incident types

---

## SOC Career Path

Typical progression:
- L1 Analyst → L2 Analyst → Senior SOC / Engineer / Incident Response / Threat Intel

Alternative paths:
- Engineering (detection / SIEM / automation)
- Incident Response (CIRT)
- Governance (GRC)
- Management / CISO track

---

## Key Mindset for SOC Analysts
- Learn from every alert
- Think like an attacker
- Validate before escalating
- Engage in incidents to build experience
