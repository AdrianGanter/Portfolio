# SOC Metrics and Objectives

## SOC Objectives

The primary objective of a Security Operations Center (SOC) is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of organisational systems and data.

SOC teams achieve this by:

* Monitoring security events
* Detecting threats
* Investigating alerts
* Escalating incidents
* Supporting remediation efforts

Effective SOC operations rely on measurable performance indicators that help assess detection capability, response efficiency, and overall security effectiveness.

---

## Core SOC Metrics

### Alert Count (AC)

Measures the total number of alerts generated and received by the SOC.

### Purpose

* Indicates analyst workload
* Highlights visibility into the environment
* Helps identify monitoring gaps or excessive alert volumes

### Considerations

* Excessively high alert volumes can overwhelm analysts
* Extremely low alert volumes may indicate detection or logging issues

---

### False Positive Rate (FPR)

Measures the percentage of alerts that do not represent genuine security threats.

### Purpose

* Evaluates detection rule quality
* Measures alert noise levels
* Identifies opportunities for tuning and automation

### Impact

A high false positive rate:

* Increases analyst workload
* Slows investigations
* Contributes to alert fatigue
* Increases the risk of missing genuine threats

### Improvement Methods

* Detection rule tuning
* SIEM optimisation
* Excluding known legitimate activity
* Automation of repetitive investigations

---

### Alert Escalation Rate (AER)

Measures how frequently alerts are escalated from L1 analysts to higher tiers.

### Purpose

* Assesses analyst confidence and experience
* Helps evaluate triage effectiveness
* Ensures appropriate use of senior analyst resources

### Considerations

* Escalating every alert creates unnecessary workload
* Escalating too few alerts may result in missed threats

---

### Threat Detection Rate (TDR)

Measures the percentage of actual threats successfully detected by the SOC.

### Purpose

* Evaluates overall detection effectiveness
* Measures visibility into adversary activity
* Identifies gaps in monitoring coverage

### Importance

Threat Detection Rate is one of the most critical SOC metrics, as undetected attacks can lead to:

* Data breaches
* Ransomware incidents
* System compromise
* Business disruption

---

## Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

SOC performance is commonly measured against Service Level Agreements (SLAs).

SLAs define expected performance targets for:

* Threat detection
* Alert acknowledgement
* Incident response

These metrics help ensure threats are handled within acceptable timeframes.

---

## Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)

Measures the time between an attack occurring and the SOC detecting it.

### Goal

Reduce the amount of time attackers can operate undetected.

### Improvement Methods

* Better detection engineering
* Faster log ingestion
* Improved visibility across systems

---

## Mean Time to Acknowledge (MTTA)

Measures how quickly an analyst begins reviewing a newly generated alert.

### Goal

Ensure timely investigation of potential threats.

### Improvement Methods

* Real-time alert notifications
* Effective workload distribution
* Clear prioritisation procedures

---

## Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)

Measures the time required to contain or remediate a confirmed threat.

### Goal

Minimise attacker impact and limit business disruption.

### Improvement Methods

* Efficient escalation processes
* Well-documented procedures
* Incident response preparedness
* Strong collaboration between SOC and IT teams

---

## Role of the L1 Analyst

L1 analysts directly influence SOC performance metrics by:

* Performing efficient alert triage
* Reducing investigation delays
* Escalating genuine threats promptly
* Producing high-quality alert reports
* Identifying excessive false positives
* Highlighting detection gaps and process improvements

While managers typically track SOC metrics, L1 analysts are often the first to identify operational issues affecting overall performance.

---

## Key Takeaways

* SOC metrics measure detection quality, efficiency, and effectiveness.
* False positives should be minimised to reduce analyst fatigue.
* Threat detection should be as close to complete as possible.
* Fast detection, acknowledgement, and response reduce attacker dwell time.
* Effective collaboration between analysts, engineers, and management improves overall SOC performance.
* Continuous improvement of metrics strengthens organisational security posture.
