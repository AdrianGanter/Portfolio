# SOC L1 Alert Reporting

## Alert Reporting Fundamentals

Alert reporting ensures investigation findings are properly documented before an alert is closed or escalated. Effective reporting provides context, preserves evidence, and allows other analysts to quickly understand the investigation without starting from scratch.

### Purpose of Alert Reporting

#### Provide Context for Escalation
- Enables L2 analysts to rapidly understand the alert.
- Reduces duplicate investigation effort.
- Supports faster incident response.

#### Preserve Investigation Findings
- Alerts and reports are often retained longer than raw logs.
- Important evidence and analyst observations remain available for future reference.

#### Improve Investigation Skills
- Writing reports reinforces understanding of the alert.
- Encourages structured analytical thinking.
- Develops communication skills required for SOC operations.

---

## The Five Ws Reporting Framework

A structured alert report should answer:

### Who
- User account involved.
- Source or destination account.
- Associated personnel or departments.

### What
- Activity that triggered the alert.
- Actions performed by the user or attacker.
- Relevant indicators observed.

### When
- Time and date of the suspicious activity.
- Duration of the activity if applicable.

### Where
- Hostname.
- Endpoint.
- IP address.
- Cloud resource or service involved.

### Why
- Investigation findings.
- Evidence supporting the verdict.
- Reasoning for escalation or closure.

---

## Alert Escalation

Escalation occurs when an alert requires further investigation, remediation, or specialist involvement beyond L1 responsibilities.

### Common Escalation Triggers

- Confirmed malicious activity.
- Potential security incidents or breaches.
- Required remediation actions.
- User account compromise.
- Malware infections.
- Cases requiring communication with other departments.
- Situations where additional expertise is required.

### Escalation Workflow

1. Review and investigate the alert.
2. Assign ownership.
3. Document findings using the Five Ws format.
4. Determine alert verdict.
5. Reassign the alert to the L2 analyst.
6. Notify the appropriate team member if required.
7. Ensure all supporting evidence is included.

---

## Communication Best Practices

### Critical Incident Escalation

If a critical incident requires immediate action:

1. Attempt contact with the assigned L2 analyst.
2. Escalate to L3 if unavailable.
3. Contact management if required.
4. Follow established incident communication procedures.

### User Validation

When validating suspicious account activity:

- Do not use potentially compromised communication channels.
- Use alternate methods such as phone calls or approved communication platforms.

### High Alert Volume

During alert surges:

- Continue prioritising based on severity and age.
- Inform senior analysts about increased workload.
- Maintain documentation standards.

### Investigation Errors

If an alert was incorrectly classified:

- Immediately notify senior analysts.
- Re-open investigation if necessary.
- Document updated findings.

### Tooling or Logging Issues

If evidence is unavailable:

- Investigate with available data.
- Document limitations.
- Report platform or logging issues to engineers or senior analysts.

# Practical Exercise 1 - Phishing Alert Investigation & Escalation

## Objective

Investigate a suspected phishing alert, document findings using the Five Ws methodology, determine the appropriate verdict, and escalate the alert according to SOC procedures.

## Investigation Process

### Step 1: Alert Review

- Opened the phishing-related alert.
- Reviewed alert description and triggering conditions.
- Identified sender information, recipient information, and associated indicators.

### Step 2: Evidence Collection

- Examined email details.
- Reviewed suspicious links, attachments, or indicators.
- Assessed whether activity matched phishing characteristics.

### Step 3: Five Ws Documentation

Documented:

#### Who
- Affected user(s).
- Email sender.

#### What
- Suspicious email activity observed.

#### When
- Timestamp of email delivery and detection.

#### Where
- Mail platform or affected account.

#### Why
- Evidence supporting the phishing assessment.

### Step 4: Verdict Assignment

- Classified the alert based on investigation findings.
- Determined whether activity represented a legitimate phishing attempt.

### Step 5: Escalation

- Assigned the alert to the designated L2 analyst.
- Saved investigation notes.
- Submitted escalation with supporting evidence.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Alert investigation.
- Email threat analysis.
- Structured report writing.
- Escalation procedures.
- Evidence documentation.

# Practical Exercise 2 - Alert Investigation & Reporting

## Objective

Investigate a second security alert, produce a detailed analyst report, determine whether escalation is required, and complete the alert lifecycle according to SOC procedures.

## Investigation Process

### Step 1: Initial Review

- Opened the alert.
- Reviewed alert description and supporting evidence.
- Identified affected systems, users, and indicators.

### Step 2: Analysis

- Examined alert fields and contextual information.
- Investigated related events.
- Assessed legitimacy of the activity.

### Step 3: Report Writing

Applied the Five Ws methodology:

#### Who
- User(s) or systems involved.

#### What
- Activity responsible for generating the alert.

#### When
- Timeline of events.

#### Where
- Affected hosts, accounts, or services.

#### Why
- Investigation findings and final reasoning.

### Step 4: Decision Making

- Determined whether the alert represented a genuine threat.
- Evaluated whether further investigation or remediation was required.

### Step 5: Closure or Escalation

Depending on findings:

- Closed the alert with documented justification, or
- Escalated to L2 with supporting evidence and investigation notes.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Alert triage.
- Evidence analysis.
- Security event investigation.
- Report writing.
- Escalation decision-making.
- SOC communication workflows.

## Key Takeaways

- Investigation quality is only valuable if findings are properly documented.
- The Five Ws framework provides a repeatable reporting methodology.
- Effective escalation allows L2 analysts to quickly continue investigations.
- Communication is critical during incidents, especially when systems, users, or other departments are involved.
- Strong reporting improves SOC efficiency, investigation quality, and incident response outcomes.
